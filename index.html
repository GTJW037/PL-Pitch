The Strategy

Four content pillars. Four to five posts per week.

Every piece of content should fall into one of these categories. This keeps the feed balanced, diverse, and always fresh — while reinforcing every revenue stream Prairie Landing has.

⛳

The Course

The main attraction. Visually stunning links-style content that makes people want to book a tee time.

  • Hole spotlights with strategy breakdowns
  • Golden hour drone footage & reels
  • Course conditions updates
  • Superintendent behind-the-scenes
  • Seasonal transformation content
🍽

McChesney's Pub

Position the restaurant as a destination — not just a 19th hole. Drive non-golfer traffic.

  • Daily specials (Taco Tues, Fish Fry Fri)
  • Chef plating & kitchen behind-the-scenes
  • Brunch setup & event prep content
  • Menu item close-ups & features
  • "You don't have to golf to eat here"
🏌️

Pro Shop & Gear

Showcase the brands and products. Drive foot traffic and position staff as knowledgeable.

  • New arrival drops & brand features
  • Staff picks of the week
  • Seasonal gear guides
  • Outfit-of-the-week styling
  • Rangefinder & tech comparisons
👥

Community & Events

Build loyalty and show the human side. Make people feel like they belong here.

  • Member spotlights & stories
  • Outing & tournament recaps
  • Wedding highlights
  • Staff features & team culture
  • User-generated content reposts
Content Format

Lead with video. The algorithm demands it.

Reels and short-form video drive 3–5x the reach of static posts on Instagram. A links course with open views, dramatic weather, and no trees blocking the shot is a video content dream.

Seasonal Calendar

Every month has a story to tell.

April
Season opener. "She's back" course reveal content. First rounds energy. Spring menu launch at McChesney's. Early bird membership push.
May
Mother's Day brunch promotion. Wedding season kicks off. Outing season begins. Long evening golden hour content. Membership spotlight.
June
Peak season ramp-up. Father's Day golf packages. Summer menu specials. Outing recaps start flowing. Longest day of the year content.

Full peak mode. Dawn tee time content. Course condition pride posts. Patio and cold drinks lifestyle content. Summer event recaps.

Late summer beauty. Beat-the-heat tips. Best-of-summer recap carousel. Pro shop back-to-fall gear. Twilight round content.

Oct
Peak fall colors on a links course. Season-end tournaments. Closing stretch energy. Best-of-year content series.
Nov–Dec
Off-season transition. Holiday events & Santa Brunch. Pro shop gift guide. Cooking classes. "See you in the spring" nostalgia. Course winterization behind-the-scenes.
Immediate Actions

Quick wins we can execute now.

These cost nothing and can start moving numbers within weeks:

  • Better photography — iPhone is fine, but shoot in golden hour and compose intentionally
  • Respond to every comment and DM — engagement breeds engagement
  • Use location tags and golf hashtags on every post
  • Cross-post McChesney's content to local food groups and pages
  • Repost any content from golfers who tag us — builds community and free content
  • Post daily Stories with weather, conditions, tee sheet availability, and specials
  • Film 15-second Reels of the course — the landscape sells itself
The Bottom Line

The product is already elite.

We don't need to build a better golf course or a better restaurant. We already have both. We need to show people — consistently, professionally, and strategically. The opportunity cost of not doing this is new customers discovering Cog Hill or Mistwood before they ever hear about us.
